Kate Middleton Wears Pink Marks &amp; Spencer Pantsuit for Emergency Call Center Visit

Publish date: 2024-09-09
  • Both Prince William and Kate Middleton visited an emergency call center in London on Thursday in order to meet with staffers working tirelessly to handle the influx of emergency calls due to the coronavirus pandemic.
  • For the visit, Duchess Kate wore a dusty pink pantsuit from Marks and Spencer and croc-embossed pointed toe pumps by Hugo Boss.

The Duchess of Cambridge remained pretty in pink while visiting the London Ambulance Service 111 control room in Croydon yesterday.

Both Duchess Kate and her husband, Prince William, visited the emergency call center to meet with staff who are answering a slew of emergency phone calls amidst the ongoing coronavirus crisis.

For the visit, Kate wore a chic, mauve-hued pantsuit—featuring a matching blazer and trousers by Autograph from Marks & Spencer—with charcoal Hugo Boss pointed-toe pumps and a cream top. Prince William opted for a monochromatic blue ensemble, donning a navy sweater with a matching suit and brown oxford shoes.

The Duke and Duchess of Cambridge recapped their visit via the Kensington Royal Instagram where they highlighted the work of the dedicated staffers and urged the public to stay home and practice social distancing as the world continues to combat the virus.

"The last few weeks, and more recent days, have been understandably concerning with the continuing spread of coronavirus. But it’s at times like this when we realize just how much the NHS represents the very best of our country and society – people from all backgrounds and walks of life with different experiences and skills, pulling together for the common good," wrote Prince William alongside the photos from their visit. "All of us have a part to play if we’re going to protect the most vulnerable. That means acting on the latest expert advice, staying home if we or those we live with have symptoms, and avoiding non-essential contact to help reduce the spread of the virus."
