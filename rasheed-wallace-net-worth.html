Rasheed Wallace | Career

Wallace started his early career playing for the University of North Carolina under coach Dean Smith.

After his impressive gameplay, Wallace got picked by the Washington Bullets as overall 4th pick in round 1 in 1995 NBA Drafts.

Meanwhile, Wallace played only one season as a rookie for the Bullets before getting traded to Portland Trail Blazers.

Surprisingly, he had a good stay and performance in the Blazers and was featured in NBA All-Star twice (2000, 2001).

Later, Rasheed moved to Atlanta Hawks but played only one game and moved to the Detroit Pistons in 2004.

Likewise, he signed a three-year contract with Boston Celtics in 2009 and helped his team reach in NBA finals.

Sadly, in 2013, he decided to hang his boots after an impressive 18 years long career in NBA.

Above all, after his retirement, Wallace got into coaching and joined Detroit Pistons as an assistant coach in 2013.

Currently, he is serving as an assistant coach for Memphis Tigers.
