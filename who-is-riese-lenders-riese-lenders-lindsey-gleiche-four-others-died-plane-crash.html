The six California residents who perished Saturday in a tiny plane crash after taking off from Las Vegas have been recognised by authorities.

Around 4:15 in the morning, a Cessna C550 business jet crashed close to French Valley Airport in Murrieta, about 80 miles southeast of Los Angeles.

Chad Bianco, the sheriff-coroner for Riverside County, described individuals who passed away

According to police, the incident happened during the second of two fog-enshrouded landing attempts. According to Elliott Simpson, an investigator with the National Transportation Safety Board, the plane, which has a maximum seating capacity of 13, crashed about 500 feet (150 metres) short of the designated runway.

The jet was discovered by deputies immersed in flames, scorching about an acre of woodland. According to a tweet from the Riverside County Fire Department, the fire was put out at 5:30 a.m.

The departure point for the trip was Las Vegas’ Harry Reid International Airport.
