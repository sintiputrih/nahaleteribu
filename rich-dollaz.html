Rich Dollaz (born August 4, 1977) is an American Music Executive and Reality TV Personality from Waterbury, Connecticut. He is famous for appearing in the reality show named “Love and Hip Hop: New York“. Furthermore, the 41-year-old executive also appeared as a supporting cast member on the first 8 seasons of TV series.

Rich also appeared as a supporting cast member in Love & Hip Hop: Hollywood Season 2. Moreover, Dollaz also cast for a minor role in the season 7th of Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta. His real full birth name is Richard A. Martin-Trowers.

Richard began his career as an intern at Bad Boys Record. It is a Sean “Diddy” Combs‘ company. In addition, Dollaz later founded a company named “Dollaz Unlimited” in 2009. Currently, he works as a CEO of the company.

He also worked on establishing the career of many artists like Yung Joc, Gorilla Zoe, and Cheri Dennis. As a music executive producer, Rich managed many new and young singers and rappers. His talent management company is working as one of the successful companies in the US.
