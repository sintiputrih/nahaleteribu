Why Was Ric Flair So Controversial in His Wrestling Career?

Publish date: 2024-04-04

In the wrestling world, Ric Flair, the "Nature Boy," is a living legend, having made his mark on the Wrestling industry for more than five decades. Nonetheless, throughout his career, he has become somewhat of a controversial figure. This has led to polarizing opinions within the wrestling community. We expand on the fraught chatter on Ric Flair and his controversy, career accolades, and what it says.


Who is Ric Flair?

Ric Flair, born Richard Morgan Fliehr on February 25, 1949, is widely regarded as one of the greatest professional wrestlers of all time, having worked for multiple organizations such as Jim Crockett Promotions (JCP), World Championship Wrestling (WCW), the World Wrestling Federation (WWF, later WWE), and Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (TNA). He is a wrestling legend widely recognized as one of many greatest pro wrestlers of his generation, and historic record time in which he hosted several titles, and awards ushering his widespread reach and influence longevity throughout his wrestling career.


Controversies

1. Racial Controversy
One of the many high-profile controversies that plagued Ric Flair was a situation involving WWE Hall of Famer Teddy Long, who stated that Flair several times called him a racial slur and threatened him. The bad blood surrounding that incident is part of the reason why Flair became a certain pariah among wrestling circles.
2. Off-screen Rivalries
Flair has had a rivalry with Bret Hart off-screen, and the allegations and derogatory claims made by him in public have only helped him develop a reputation for controversy. Hart criticized Flair's abilities, use of ring psychology, and what he believed was Flair's poor blade job.

In-ring Prowess

Controversies aside, there's no question Flair still knows how to shine in the ring. Diverging Perspectives

For some fans, Flair is a polarizing figure, in part because of the personal conduct and rivalries that have accompanied his career; however, others regard him as one of the greatest professional wrestlers of all time and the only wrestler to hold 16 championships.
