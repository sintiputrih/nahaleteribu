Career

Journalism

In February 2006, Kamiya Jani started her career as a journalist with The Times of India. In June 2006, Kamiya switched her job and joined CNBC TV18 as a Correspondent and Sub-editor.

Kamiya Jani with her colleague at CNBC

She served at CNBC TV18 for a period of 1.8 years. Later, in February 2008, Kamiya joined Bloomberg UTV and worked there as an Anchor and Associate Producer for a peroid of 2 years. Her work included anchoring and producing lifestyle, travel, and entertainment shows. In February 2010, Kamiya worked as a Financial Market Anchor with ET Now and Travel Anchor with MagicBricks Now.

Kamiya Jani anchoring a show on ET Now

YouTube

In 2016, Kamiya Jani started her own blog Curly Tales on YouTube. In an interview, talking about quitting her stable job, Kamiya said,

I got bored with the same routine of waking up at 5 am for a TV show and decided to take the plunge. I realised there weren’t any platforms dedicated to local discovery, travel and recommendations. So I decided to quit my job and put all my energy into Curly Tales.”

Initially, Kamiya started with a 1-minute text-based video. Later, she started posting food and travel blogs on YouTube. Kamiya Jani hosts various shows on her Youtube channel like; Sunday Brunch, a celebrity talk show wherein she invites renowned personalities for a brunch, Tere Gully Mein, Street Stories, Curly Tales in UAE, and many more.

A still from Kamiya Jani’s show Sunday Brunch

Entrepreneur

Kamiya Jani is the founder and Editor-in-Chief of Curly Tales, which is a food, travel, experience, and lifestyle content platform. It is a recommendation platform to let people discover their interest in food and travel.  Curly Tales is a team of more than 20 people with its head office situated in Mumbai. In an interview, talking about her journey with Curly Tales, Kamiya said,

Curly tales is a food and travel platform and thankfully all age groups enjoy these two categories. That’s the whole intent of reaching out to various age groups. How it originated is an interesting story. 4-5 years ago, I was a news anchor at a business news channel but after a good 10 years of corporate job in media industry I decided to listen to my inner calling. I quit my job, started travelling, documenting my travels and culinary journeys, made videos posted on Facebook and boom I saw some really good response coming from all age groups. So that’s how Curly tales was born back in 2017, cut to 2021 we are now with 2.1 million followers on Facebook.”
