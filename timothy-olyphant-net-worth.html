After completing his degree, he was attracted to acting in New York and began studying at the William Esper Studio.

Career

Timothy Olyphant began acting off-Broadway, making his professional debut in 1995 in the Playwrights Horizons’ production of The Monogamist as Tim Hapgood.

Olyphant relocated to Los Angeles and began working in the television industry. In 1995, he scored his first TV role in a pilot for Clint Eastwood’s adaptation of the series 77 Sunset Strip.

He played Scooby, Mr. Smith’s (Scott Bakula) assistant, in Mr. & Mrs. Smith’s (1996) pilot, a short-lived CBS spy series.

In 1997, Olyphant starred in three episodes of the critically acclaimed police drama “High Incident” and the Hallmark Channel TV film “Ellen Foster”.

In 1998, he had a guest leading part in the HBO film “When Trumpets Fade”, a recreation of the 1944 Battle of Hürtgen Forest, and then a guest-starring role in the Sex and the City episode “Valley of the Twenty-Some Guys.”

He joined the cast of FX’s Damages, starring Glenn Close, in June 2008. As a result, his role in “Samantha Who?” was recast, and Billy Zane was cast in his place.

In 2009, he co-starred in the thriller “A Perfect Getaway” alongside Steve Zahn and Milla Jovovich as an Iraq war veteran and suspected serial killer of Hawaii honeymooners.

In late November 2008, he signed on for the starring role of the local sheriff in the 2010 picture “The Crazies.”
