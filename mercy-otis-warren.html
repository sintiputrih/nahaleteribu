Biography

Mercy Otis Warren was born in Barnstable, MA on September 14, 1728. An American Revolutionary-era author of history, propaganda, and political satire, she is best known for her multi-volume work, History of the Rise, Progress, and Termination of the American Revolution (1805). This was the first historical account of the American Revolution to be written by a woman. She and her husband were close friends of Abigail and John Adams. She received no formal education, but studied informally with a tutor who worked for her brothers. From her early hood, she moved in influential circles; her friends included John Hancock, Martha and George Washington, Samuel Adams, Patrick Henry, and Thomas Jefferson.
