Publish date: 2024-03-08

The professional basketball player Jordan Poole’s parents take immense pride in his accomplishments and abilities.

As the newest addition to the Washington Wizards, Jordan Poole shares a strong and close bond with his parents, often seen in pictures together.

Furthermore, both of Jordan’s parents have a background in athletics themselves.

The Golden State Warriors drafted Jordan as the 28th overall pick in the 2019 NBA draft.

Jordan Poole made his debut in the NBA in the opening game of the 2019-20 NBA season, with the Golden State Warriors facing off against the Los Angeles Clippers.

Despite being just 24 year old, he has already won the NBA championship when the Warriors secured victory during the 2022 season.

Before making it in the NBA, Jordan played as a point guard and shooting guard for the Michigan Wolverines during college.

Who Are Jordan Poole Parents? 

The Washington Wizards point guard Jordan Poole is the son of Anthony Poole and Monet Poole. He grew up with two sisters and a brother.

Jordan hails from a family with a solid athletic background, as many of his known relatives are involved in sports.

Both of Jordan Poole’s parents were athletes themselves in high school.

Anthony Poole was an exceptional high school athlete at Simeone Career Academy in Chicago.

Later, he played college football at the University of Wisconsin-Whitewater.

On the other hand, Monet Poole was actively involved in sports at Washington High School in Milwaukee and attended the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee.

They first met while in college and eventually settled down in Milwaukee.

Throughout Jordan’s basketball journey, both Monet and Anthony played crucial roles in supporting and guiding him.

Anthony also took to train his son whenever he could, and Jordan could be seen accompanying him in the gym from an early age.

Alongside coaching his son, Anthony worked as a UPS driver and eventually became a manager.

Meanwhile, Jordan’s mother, Monet, pursued a career as a case manager and social worker at the Eisenhower Center in Milwaukee.
