Joyner Lucas | Career

In 2007, Joyner Lucas created a group with his cousin Cyrus tha Great and released a mixtape named ‘Workprint: The Greatest Mixtape of All Time.’ 

Joyner first released his solo mixtape, ‘Listen to Me’ in 2011, followed by ‘LFO’s (Low-Frequency Oscillators)’ in 2013. 

In 2015, he released another mixtape, ‘Along Came Joyner’, which had his critically acclaimed song “Ross Capicchioni.” 

After the song’s success, he was signed to Atlantic Records in 2016, from where he released his fourth mixtape, ‘508-507-2209‘.

He was rumored to earn $1 million for the album. 

His career took another step when he released his 2017-hit song, ‘I’m Not Racist.’ The song quickly went viral and raised many conversations as well as debates regarding race. 

In 2018, Joyner teamed up with Chris Brown to announce their collaborative album ‘Angels & Demons.’

The same year, he was also featured in Eminem’s song, ‘Lucky You.’ The song did really well as it debuted at No. 6 in Billboard Hot 100.

Joyner released his debut studio album ‘ADHD’ in 2020 that features artists like Chris Brown, Timbaland, Fabolous, Logic, King OSF, and Young Thug. 

After that, he released his debut EP, ‘Evolution,’ with 12 tracks.
