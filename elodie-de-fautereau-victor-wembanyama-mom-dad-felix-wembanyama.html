Meet Elodie de Fautereau & Felix Wembanyama, Victor Wembanyama’s Parents

Victor was born on January 4, 2004, in Le Chesnay, France, to his parents, Elodie de Fautereau & Felix Wembanyama as a second child.

His parents raised him in an athletic environment, eventually directing him toward this line of sports. His initial introduction to the sports world was soccer, as he grew up playing the game and also was an excellent player in the local tournaments. He didn’t excel only in basketball but also was an outstanding goalkeeper in soccer.

Athletism runs in the blood of the Wembanyama family. Father also competed in various sporting events during his prime representing France in international tournaments. Felix Wembanyama is French-born with Congolese ancestry. He used to compete in high jumps and long jumps for track and field.

Victor’s parents, especially his father, urged him to concentrate on track before basketball because he wanted to see his son compete on a vast platform and carry the family legacy forward. However, he gravitated toward basketball as his main interest in sports, and his choice has worked out perfectly. The level of success he is currently at in basketball would be tough to surpass in any other sport, although he may have also thrived in the athletic area.

Since he was four or five years old, his mother, a former player, has taught Wembanyama the basics of basketball. She is a very athletic woman who stands 6 feet 3 inches tall. He has inherited these qualities from his parents, which have undoubtedly assisted him in his game.

The administration of significant teams in Spain began to hear about his brilliance. He started his professional career with Entente Le Chesnay Versailles, a local club, before enrolling in Nanterre 92’s youth program when he was eleven. But his parents declined offers from more illustrious organizations like ASVEL and FC Barcelona. Wembanyama led his squad to third place in the Minicopa del Rey, an event for young athletes under 14, while on loan to FC Barcelona in February 2018.

Who Are Victor Wembanyama’s Siblings?

Victor is the second child of his parents, so he has an elder sister and a younger brother as siblings.

They all grew together in Le Chesney, France, which they call home. All kids of the Wembanyama family are active in basketball and pushing their family name to prestigious heights, setting an example for the much younger generations.

Eve Wembanyama, Victor’s older sister, is a basketball player who is presently a small forward for the French LDLC team ASVEL Feminine. She was a member of the French squad that won the gold medal in the FIBA U16 Women’s European Championship and is 6 feet tall. Like her brother, she is also considered one of the excellent women’s talents in the France basketball scene.

Recently, she equaled a career-best on January 29, 2022, in a France – LF2 matchup. She contributed 13 points on that particular day in Calais’s 73-66 road loss to Aulnoye. She also had two steals and six rebounds. She shot 44.4% overall and 2/7 from two and two from three. She also made 3/4 of her free throw attempts.

Oscar Wembanyama, Victor’s younger brother, is making waves in the French basketball scene. Recently, the 15-year-old signed with ASVEL’s U18 squad. With this chance, the youthful Wembanyama, who has already distinguished himself with a few outstanding shots on the side of the U15 Elite of Nanterre, will have plenty of time to develop his basketball. Victor’s brother is now waiting for the next level, and if he has the skills, maybe even a spot on the squad of the French champions in a few years.
