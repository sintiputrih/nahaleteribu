Biography

Misty Copeland was born in Kansas City, MO on September 10, 1982. Ballet dancer who became the first African-American soloist in the history of the American Ballet Theatre, eventually becoming the first African-American woman to serve as the company’s principal dancer. She performed in The Chocolate Nutcracker, which was narrated by Debbie Allen. She studied at the San Francisco Ballet School after studying gymnastics and dance as a child.
